## 2. The Identity Diff

### A. Ownership vs. Borrowing
- **Office**: Identity is tied to the **Project**. A project "owns" its source, lockfiles, and environment.
- **Campus**: Identity is tied to the **Domain**. Knowledge is a shared asset that agents **Borrow**. Identity is non-permanent and expires with the task lifetime.

### B. Execution vs. Reflection
- **Office**: The "Borders" are defined by **Build Orders** (`shared-types` -> `shared-resilience` -> `app`).
- **Campus**: The "Borders" are defined by **Cognitive Lenses** (Patterns, Routines, Profiles). Movement is not a build step, but a "Rotation" of context.

### C. Enforcement vs. Orchestration
- **Office**: Boundaries are enforced by **TUV-001** (Safety/Security).
- **Campus**: Boundaries are orchestrated by **The Board of Governors** (Fidelity/Subject Matter).

## 3. The "Hyperspace" Interface
The boundary between these two worlds is **Hyperspace**. This is where:
1.  **Input** from the Office (Signals) is received.
2.  **Lambda (λ)** from the Campus is applied.
3.  **Output** (The Active Landing) is determined.

## 4. Identity Synthesis
The **Structure Identity** of the Campus is that of a **High-Fidelity Feedback Loop**. It exists to ingest the "Action" of the Office, borrow the "Knowledge" of the Library, and produce a "Pattern" that can be graduated back into the source.
